#### Ironing

**What it does:** After printing a top surface, the nozzle makes an extra pass over it with very little filament, smoothing out any lines or gaps.

**Why it matters:** Ironing produces noticeably smoother, flatter top surfaces that look almost polished. It is great for models where the top face is highly visible.

**Typical values:** Enabled or disabled. When on, it typically uses 10-15% flow and a fine spacing of 0.1 mm between passes.

**Tip:** Only enable ironing when top surface appearance matters -- it adds print time for every top layer and is not needed for functional parts or hidden surfaces.
