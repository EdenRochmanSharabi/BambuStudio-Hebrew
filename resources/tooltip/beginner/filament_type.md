#### Filament Type

**What it does:** Tells the slicer which material you are printing with so it can apply the correct temperature, speed, and cooling settings.

**Why it matters:** Different materials behave very differently -- PLA is easy and forgiving, PETG is stronger and heat-resistant, ABS is durable but warps easily, and TPU is flexible. Using the wrong settings for a material can cause failed prints.

**Typical values:** PLA (beginner-friendly), PETG (stronger, slight flex), ABS (tough, needs enclosure), TPU (flexible/rubbery).

**Tip:** Start with PLA -- it is the easiest material to print, requires no heated enclosure, and works well for the vast majority of projects.
