#### Support Threshold Angle

**What it does:** Sets the minimum overhang angle (measured from vertical) at which support structures will automatically be generated.

**Why it matters:** A lower angle generates supports only for very steep overhangs, while a higher angle adds supports more aggressively. Getting this right avoids wasting material on supports that are not needed while still protecting parts that need them.

**Typical values:** 45 degrees is the standard starting point. Most printers handle up to 45 degrees without support.

**Tip:** Keep this at 45 degrees to start. If you notice drooping on gentler slopes, lower the angle by 5 degrees at a time.
