#### Retraction

**What it does:** Briefly pulls the filament backward into the nozzle before the printhead travels to a new location, then pushes it back out when printing resumes.

**Why it matters:** Without retraction, melted filament oozes out of the nozzle during travel moves, leaving thin strings and blobs on your print. Proper retraction keeps moves clean.

**Typical values:** 0.5-1.0 mm retraction length for direct-drive extruders (like Bambu Lab printers), 4-6 mm for Bowden setups. Speed: 30-60 mm/s.

**Tip:** If you see thin strings between parts of your print, slightly increase retraction length by 0.2 mm at a time until the stringing stops.
