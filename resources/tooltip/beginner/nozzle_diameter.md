#### Nozzle Diameter

**What it does:** Tells the slicer the size of your printer's nozzle opening, which determines the width of each extruded line.

**Why it matters:** A smaller nozzle prints finer detail but takes much longer. A larger nozzle prints faster and stronger but cannot reproduce small features as precisely.

**Typical values:** 0.4 mm (standard, great all-rounder), 0.2 mm (fine detail), 0.6 mm (faster prints), 0.8 mm (fast and strong).

**Tip:** Stick with the 0.4 mm nozzle that comes with your printer -- it handles the widest range of models well and has the most community support.
